# Teacher Review Guide (Grade 7 Readiness)

This document gives curriculum owners a consistent way to review each module for 7th‑grade readiness.
Use it alongside the module content and assessments.

## Rubric: 7th‑Grade Readiness

**Score each criterion as:** ✅ Meets • ⚠️ Needs work • ❌ Missing

### 1) Vocabulary load
- **Target:** 6–10 new terms per module, introduced gradually and reused.
- **Check for:** clear definitions, bolded or highlighted terms, and a quick recap box.
- **Common issues:** too many terms in one lesson, definitions that assume prior chemistry knowledge.

### 2) Prerequisite assumptions
- **Target:** assumes basic middle‑school math (ratios, simple graphs) and everyday science.
- **Check for:** explicit reminders of prior knowledge (mass vs. weight, particle model, states of matter).
- **Common issues:** using formulas without explanation, assuming lab safety knowledge.

### 3) Misconceptions
- **Target:** at least 1–2 misconceptions addressed per module.
- **Check for:** a callout like “Common misunderstanding” + a short correction.
- **Common issues:** confusing heat with temperature, atoms vs. molecules, density vs. mass.

### 4) Examples & analogies
- **Target:** 2–3 concrete examples tied to everyday life (kitchen, water, sports, weather).
- **Check for:** examples that match the science and avoid misleading metaphors.
- **Common issues:** analogies that introduce incorrect causal explanations.

## Standard “Review Agent” Prompt Template (Per Module)

> **Role:** You are a curriculum reviewer for a 7th‑grade chemistry course.
>
> **Module:** [Module number + title]
>
> **Task:** Evaluate the module for 7th‑grade readiness. Use the rubric below.
>
> **Rubric focus:** vocabulary load, prerequisite assumptions, misconceptions, examples/analogies.
>
> **Output format:**
> 1) **Rubric ratings** (✅/⚠️/❌) with 1–2 sentences each.
> 2) **Checklist** (Pass/Needs work + specific fixes).
> 3) **Top 3 priority fixes** (short bullets).
>
> **Constraints:** Keep feedback actionable, cite specific module sections, and avoid adding new topics.
